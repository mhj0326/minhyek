ROBOTIS

Elevator Recognition software for Indoor Autonomous Robots: Data Augmentation and Optimization

The project aimed to develop a robust elevator recognition system tailored for indoor autonomous robots, addressing critical challenges such as motion blur from robot movement, diverse lighting conditions, and the wide variability of elevator indicators across buildings.

The software was designed to enhance navigation and operational flexibility by accurately detecting elevator states, including current floor, door status, direction of travel, and load status. To achieve these goals, the project employed a hierarchical two-stage detection model. In the first stage, YOLOv7 identifies broad object classes such as floor indicators, doors, and buttons. In the second stage, CNNs perform fine-grained classification, detecting specific states like whether the door is open or closed, or the exact floor level displayed.

To improve detection performance, the project implemented data augmentation methods. One approach, patch augmentation, involved dividing images into smaller patches to better detect small objects, such as numerical or textual indicators. This method expanded the dataset size from 5,000 to 28,000 images, leading to a significant improvement in accuracy.

Another method, diffusion augmentation, utilized diffusion models to inpaint underrepresented classes, such as specific floor indicators or directional signs. This process helped balance the dataset and further enhanced model performance, resulting in notable metric improvements, including an increase in mAP@0.5 from 0.730 to 0.784, as well as enhanced recall and precision.

The project also focused on domain adaptation and fine-tuning. Models pretrained on a diverse set of seven locations performed significantly better than those trained on standard datasets like COCO. Fine-tuning these pretrained models with additional location-specific data led to further gains in performance, proving effective for new, unseen environments. It was also observed that increasing the amount of fine-tuning data provided incremental performance improvements, though diminishing returns were noted beyond a certain dataset size.

The system was developed on an Orin AGX platform, using ROS2 Foxy and wide-angle FHD cameras to address field-of-view constraints. Camera positioning and angles were carefully optimized to capture all relevant indicators within the elevator environment.

The demo test for the software enabling robots to detect and operate elevator buttons went successful, and the software is now being used in the ROBOTIS GAEMI Robot product.
